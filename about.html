About Me

For swiftly o’er the level shore
The waves of progress ride;
The ancient landmarks one by one Shall sink beneath the tide.
Time-honored creeds and ancient faith, The Altar and the Crown,
Lordship’s hereditary right,
Before that tide go down.
Base churl shall mock the mighty names Writ on the roll of time;
Religion shall be held a jest
And loyalty a crime.
No word of prayer, no hymns of praise Sound in the village school;
The people’s education
Utilitarians rule.
In England’s ancient pulpits
Lay orators shall preach
New creeds, and free religions
Self-made apostles teach.
No harvest feast nor Christmastide Shall farm or manor hold;
Science alone can plenty give,
The only God is Gold.

-- Excerpt: The Fox's Prophecy By D.W. Nash, 1871
